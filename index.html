Crypto currency, such as Bitcoin, is a decentralized form of digital currency that uses cryptography for secure transactions.
It operates on a technology called blockchain, which is a public ledger that records all transactions.
Crypto currencies offer secure and anonymous transactions, eliminating the need for intermediaries and reducing costs.
They have gained popularity for their potential for financial inclusivity and investment opportunities.
However, challenges such as price volatility, regulatory frameworks, and security risks remain. Despite these challenges,
crypto currency continues to evolve and gain acceptance in various sectors, indicating its growing significance in the financial landscape.
